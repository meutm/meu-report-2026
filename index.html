Section 01
Event Overview
Context, purpose, and institutional positioning of MEU Timișoara 2026.

Model European Union Timișoara 2026 was the first MEU conference in Romania at this scale - and the first in a city whose democratic history makes it a uniquely appropriate venue. Timișoara, which ignited the 1989 Romanian Revolution, hosted its first European legislative simulation as part of the prestigious MEU conference series previously held in Spain, the Netherlands, Germany, Portugal, and Poland.

The event brought together 70 students and young professionals from 15 countries over five days, placing them in the roles of Members of the European Parliament and Council Ministers. The two chambers debated and voted on real EU legislative proposals, following authentic procedural rules.

The event was co-funded by the European Union via the Erasmus+ programme and held under the High Patronage of the European Parliament - the first time a Romanian MEU has received this distinction.

Section 11
Sustainability & Responsible Practices
MEU Timișoara 2026 embedded environmental and ethical responsibility across every operational dimension.
100% Digital Participant Journey
9 web platforms replaced all printed materials - guides, programmes, certificates, agreements, and position papers. Zero paper waste from the event's information lifecycle.
100% Recyclable Physical Items
Participant gifts - bamboo thermos bottles - were fully recyclable and sourced as sustainably produced items. All physical touchpoints were designed with end-of-life in mind.
Rainforest Alliance Certified Food
Food products consumed during the event carried the Rainforest Alliance certification (Green Frog label), confirming sustainable sourcing, environmental standards, and respect for farmers' rights.
Public Transport Integration
The participant guide app included a dedicated section with exact public transport routes, stops, and timetables from both accommodation sites to all event venues - actively lowering the barrier to sustainable travel within Timișoara.
Responsible AI Usage
AI tools were permitted exclusively for brainstorming and ideation phases. All amendments, position papers, and final legislative texts were written, argued, and voted on by participants themselves - preserving the authentic educational and democratic value of the simulation.
Made in Europe Products
All physical products and materials used in the event were sourced from European manufacturers - supporting the single market and maintaining supply chain transparency aligned with the event's European values.
Participant Feedback on Sustainability

One participant's open response explicitly requested a commitment be renewed for future editions: "DO NOT PRINT - I do not support waste. Also, since it is an Erasmus project, you should remind all participants not to waste food - there were a lot of food waste moments. You should trigger awareness on this, because Erasmus itself is based on sustainability." This represents actionable feedback for 2027 operations.

Section 12
Strategic Recommendations
Synthesised from Q49 (Secretary-General change) and open qualitative responses. Prioritised by frequency of mention and operational feasibility.
Priority 1 · Most Frequently Cited
Distribute directive texts before the event - with sufficient lead time
This was the dominant structural critique, mentioned directly in 6+ responses and identified as the single highest-priority change by multiple Q49 responses. Participants arrived without access to the full legislative texts, forcing improvised position papers based on general knowledge rather than the specific document. Recommendation: distribute full directive texts, procedural rules, and a written EU institutional guide at minimum 3 weeks before the event.
Priority 2 · First-Timer Onboarding
Dedicated preparation pathway for first-time simulation participants
40% of respondents were at their first-ever simulation experience. Several described a steep learning curve that resolved only partway through the event. A structured onboarding module - procedural walkthrough, role-specific guide, or mandatory mock session - would close this gap significantly and raise the baseline quality of debate in the first rounds.
Priority 3 · Simulation Technology
Real-time vote display screens and expanded Press Corps
Multiple respondents requested voting screens displaying MEP/Minister votes in real time - as in the actual European Parliament - to increase procedural authenticity and dramatic engagement. A secondary recommendation: expand the Press Corps to include dedicated reporters for both chambers simultaneously, with real-time reporting shared to group channels.
Priority 4 · Safety & Inclusion
Increase Safe Person mechanism visibility and introduce anonymous reporting
27% of feedback respondents were unaware of the Safe Person mechanism during the event. Recommendations: (a) explicit introduction at the opening ceremony, (b) visible Safe Person identification throughout all sessions, (c) anonymous per-session or per-group reporting form accessible via the participant app.
Additional Operational Improvements Identified
Single accommodation venue for all participants Programme backup activities for unplanned delays Cross-chamber ice-breakers (EP ↔ Council) More legal advisors in-session Closing shared dinner / final social gathering More diverse political topic selection Earlier confirmation of accommodation assignments Food waste awareness briefing (Erasmus alignment)
Context for 2027

Across all qualitative responses, a consistent subtext emerges: "the mistakes were expected for a first edition - but they should not be repeated." This is the precise frame the organising team should adopt for 2027 planning. The structural quality of the event - venues, programme design, speaker quality, digital infrastructure, meals, and the simulation itself - was broadly excellent. The improvements that matter are operational and communicational: distribute materials earlier, onboard first-timers more deliberately, and keep the group together.

Key Amendments Adopted - Schengen Military Corridor
Art. 3(4–6) - New
Prohibition on internal use + disaster relief
Corridor explicitly prohibited for internal political conflicts or domestic unrest. Disaster relief activation permitted under Art. 222 TFEU, subject to ERCC notification and UCPM coordination.
Art. 6a - New
Special Fund for Infrastructural Investment
Commission shall establish a dedicated Union financing instrument for dual-use infrastructure within 9 months. Covers bottleneck removal, logistical hubs, rail, bridges, ports. Annual reporting to EP and Council required.
Art. 8b - New
Corridor Stress Tests & Mobility Exercises
Biannual testing of military mobility corridors through physical movement or digital simulations. Simulates crisis conditions; assessed bottlenecks must be addressed at national and Union level.
Art. 8a - Expanded
Enhanced Coordination Mode - Extended scope
Added: maritime port and private rail priority for military logistics; heightened surveillance against hybrid threats and cyber-interference; immediate notification to national parliaments and EP upon activation.
Art. 11(3) - New
Intelligence Coordination Unit (EUMS)
Dedicated unit within EU Military Staff to monitor transit flows, produce threat assessments, and share declassified intelligence with INTCEN and national authorities. Classified under RESTREINT UE / SECRET UE framework.
Art. 6 - Amended
Infrastructure: sustainability + MLC standards
Dual-use infrastructure must minimise greenhouse gas emissions and avoid biodiversity loss. Explicit alignment with Military Load Classifications (MLC). Equitable access for all MS, priority to cohesion regions.

Key Amendments Adopted - MyHealth@EU
Art. 12 - Fully Revised
Granular consent + patient dashboard
Consent must be granular (specific data categories, named recipients, purpose, duration). Centralised patient dashboard for real-time viewing, modification and revocation. Immutable time-stamped audit logs accessible at all times.
Art. 14 - Expanded
Binding encryption + independent cybersecurity audits
Binding encryption rules for data in transit and at rest. Independent expert audits and stress-testing. Commission must ensure transparency on data centre location, ownership and jurisdictional exposure. EU-preferential data storage.
Art. 24 - New
Green Technologies
Digital infrastructures supporting MyHealth@EU shall use energy-efficient data centres, renewable energy, low-impact cooling, and circular economy practices. Service providers to report energy consumption and carbon emissions.
Art. 11(1) - Amended
Mental health data in essential records
Mental health conditions, psychiatric treatment and current medication explicitly included in essential health data - subject to strict data protection and consent safeguards under GDPR and this Directive.
Art. 6(3) - Amended
15-day response deadline (non-emergency)
Vague "reasonable timeframe" replaced with a binding maximum of 15 working days for non-emergency requests. Emergency responses must be kept to a minimum - timeline to be determined by medical experts.
Art. 23(4) + Art. 8(5) - New
Offline access + vulnerability-inclusive design
Member States must provide offline or human-assisted access points. Information design must serve elderly, low-digital-literacy and disadvantaged socioeconomic groups - including non-digital support and easy-to-read financial guidance.

Both directives were adopted on 29 March 2026 in Timișoara. The final texts reflect the outcome of debates, points of information, and amendment votes conducted across both chambers under standard EU ordinary legislative procedure rules.
